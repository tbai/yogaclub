## 1. Welcome to Yogi Club

### Our Mission

Yogi Club brings yoga, mindfulness, and movement to young children in a fun, nurturing, and playful environment. We believe every child deserves tools to feel calm, confident, and connected to their body.

### Our Philosophy

- **Play and have fun with kids yoga.** Children learn through play. Every pose is a story, every sequence is an adventure.
- **No perfection.** There is no "wrong" way to do a pose. Effort and joy matter more than alignment.
- **Inclusion always.** Every child participates at their own level. We adapt, not exclude.
- **Connection over instruction.** Build relationships with the children. They follow people they trust.

### Requirements to Be a Yogi Club Kids Yoga Teacher

**Essential (non-negotiable):**
- **Working With Children Check (WWCC)** — This is mandatory. No WWCC, no teaching. No exceptions.

**Highly recommended:**
- **irst Aid Certificate** — A current First Aid course (including CPR) is strongly recommended. When working with young children, knowing how to respond to an emergency is critical.
- **Kids Yoga Teacher Training** — A certified kids yoga training course gives you the tools, techniques, and confidence to lead classes effectively. We recommend completing at least a foundational kids yoga training.

**The most important qualities:**
- **Motivation** — You genuinely want to make a difference in children's lives
- **Energy** — Kids feed off your energy. If you bring it, they'll match it. If you're flat, the class will be flat.
- **Creativity** — Every class is different. You need to think on your feet, make up stories, invent games, and adapt in the moment.
- **Love for kids** — This one can't be taught. You either light up around children or you don't.

> Certificates and courses are important, but at the end of the day, a motivated, energetic, and creative teacher who truly loves working with kids will always outshine someone with all the qualifications but no spark.

### What Makes Kids Yoga Different from Adult Yoga

| Adult Yoga | Kids Yoga |
|---|---|
| Quiet, introspective | Loud, interactive, playful |
| Hold poses for minutes | Hold poses for seconds |
| Verbal cues | Visual demos + storytelling |
| Structured sequences | Flexible, theme-based |
| Internal focus | External engagement (games, songs) |
| Stillness is expected | Movement is expected |

## 2. Understanding Child Development (Ages 1–6)

### Age 1–2 (Toddlers)

**Physical:** Learning to walk, unsteady balance, limited coordination.
**Cognitive:** Short attention span (1–3 minutes per activity). Learning through imitation.
**Social:** Parallel play (next to, not with, other children). Separation anxiety common.

**Yoga approach:**
- Parent/carer participation is essential
- Simple movements: stretching arms up, touching toes, rocking
- Use songs and rhythm — they respond to music more than words
- Sessions: 15 minutes maximum
- Lots of repetition — they love doing the same thing over and over
- Use props: scarves, soft toys, bubbles

### Age 2–3 (Older Toddlers)

**Physical:** Better balance, can jump, climb, run. Still developing fine motor skills.
**Cognitive:** Attention span 3–5 minutes. Beginning to understand simple instructions. Love animals.
**Social:** Starting to play alongside others. "Mine!" phase. Big emotions.

**Yoga approach:**
- Animal poses are gold (dog, cat, frog, snake)
- Simple breathing: blow out candles, smell the flowers
- Start introducing partner work (with parent/carer)
- Sessions: 15–20 minutes
- Use stuffed animals as yoga buddies
- Keep transitions quick — they lose interest fast

### Age 3–4 (Preschoolers)

**Physical:** Good gross motor skills, can balance on one foot briefly, hop, gallop.
**Cognitive:** Attention span 5–8 minutes. Love stories and pretend play. Asking "why?" constantly.
**Social:** Beginning cooperative play. Learning to share and take turns.

**Yoga approach:**
- Story-based classes work brilliantly (yoga adventures)
- Can follow 2–3 step instructions
- Introduce breathing exercises with props (pinwheels, feathers)
- Start simple partner poses
- Sessions: 20–30 minutes
- They can start remembering pose names
- Theme-based classes (jungle, ocean, space)

### Age 4–5 (Pre-Kindy)

**Physical:** Good coordination, can balance longer, starting to skip.
**Cognitive:** Attention span 8–12 minutes. Understand rules. Creative and imaginative.
**Social:** Cooperative play, friendships forming, can work in small groups.

**Yoga approach:**
- More complex sequences and flows
- Introduce yoga games with rules
- Partner and group poses
- Mindfulness activities (body scan, gratitude)
- Sessions: 20 minutes
- Can learn Sun Salutation with animal names
- Challenge poses (tree, eagle) — they love trying hard things

### Age 5–6 (Kindy/Prep)

**Physical:** Strong coordination, can hold balance poses, beginning flexibility awareness.
**Cognitive:** Attention span 12–15 minutes. Understand sequences. Can reflect on feelings.
**Social:** Strong friendships, group dynamics, beginning empathy.

**Yoga approach:**
- Full yoga sequences and flows
- Mindfulness and meditation (guided visualisation, 2–3 minutes)
- Journaling or drawing after relaxation
- Yoga challenges and obstacle courses
- Sessions: 20–30 minutes
- Introduce concepts like kindness, gratitude, courage through yoga
- They can lead parts of the class (be the teacher!)

## 3. Class Structure & Flow

### Universal Class Structure (Adapt timing by age)

Every class follows this arc: **Arrive → Move → Breathe → Rest**

#### 1. Opening Circle (3–5 min)
**Purpose:** Settle in, create connection, set the tone.

- Sit in a circle together
- Welcome song or greeting ritual (same every class — kids love routine)
- "How are you feeling today?" — use a feelings chart for younger ones
- Introduce the theme: "Today we're going on a jungle adventure!"
- Ring a singing bowl or chime to signal "yoga time"

**Opening ritual ideas:**
- Pass a "talking stone" — only the person holding it speaks
- "Namaste" hands + bow together
- Sing the Yogi Club welcome song
- Three deep breaths together with a bell

#### 2. Warm-Up (3–5 min)
**Purpose:** Get bodies moving, release energy, prepare muscles.

- Shaking: shake hands, feet, whole body, then freeze
- Dancing: free dance to music, freeze when music stops
- Animal walks: bear walk, crab walk, frog jumps across the room
- Simple stretches: reach to the sky, touch the ground, twist side to side
- "Follow the leader" — teacher does movements, kids copy

#### 3. Yoga Adventure / Main Activity (10–20 min)
**Purpose:** Teach poses through story, theme, or sequence.

This is the heart of the class. Structure options:

**A) Story-Based (best for ages 2–4)**
Tell a story and do poses as characters/scenes appear:
> "We're walking through the jungle (marching)... we see a tall tree (Tree Pose)... a snake slithers by (Cobra)... we hear a lion roar (Lion's Breath)..."

**B) Sequence-Based (best for ages 4–6)**
Teach a flow or series of connected poses:
- Kids' Sun Salutation
- Animal flow (Cat → Cow → Dog → Cobra → Frog)
- Seasonal flow (falling leaves, snowflakes, sunshine)

**C) Station/Game-Based (all ages)**
Set up activity stations or play yoga games (see Section 5).

**Tips for the main activity:**
- Demonstrate every pose — don't just describe it
- Use the pose's fun name AND the animal/object name
- Hold poses 3–5 breaths (count out loud for young ones)
- Give positive reinforcement: "Look at your strong tree!" not "Good job"
- Offer modifications: "If you want more challenge, try closing your eyes"
- Keep it moving — if energy drops, switch activities

#### 4. Breathing Exercises (3–5 min)
**Purpose:** Transition from active to calm. Teach self-regulation.

- Place hands on belly to feel breath
- Use a prop (see breathing exercises below)
- Practice 3–5 rounds of one technique
- For young ones, keep it playful and short

#### 5. Relaxation / Savasana (3–5 min)
**Purpose:** Rest, integrate, develop stillness skills.

- Dim lights if possible
- Play soft music
- Children lie down (offer eye pillows or soft toys on belly)
- Guided relaxation or story
- For toddlers: just lying quietly with a teddy for 1 minute is a win
- For 5–6 year olds: guided body scan or visualisation

#### 6. Closing Circle (2–3 min)
**Purpose:** Reflect, connect, close the practice.

- Sit back in circle
- "What was your favourite pose today?"
- Closing song or ritual
- "Namaste" together
- Ring the singing bowl

### Timing Guide by Age

| Age | Total Class | Opening | Warm-Up | Main | Breathing | Relaxation | Closing |
|-----|-------------|---------|---------|------|-----------|------------|---------|
| 0–2 (Baby) | 15 min | 2 min | 2 min | 6 min | 1 min | 2 min | 2 min |
| 2–3 (Toddler) | 15–20 min | 2 min | 3 min | 6–8 min | 2 min | 2 min | 2 min |
| 4–6 (Pre-Kindy / Kindy) | 20–30 min | 3 min | 4 min | 10–15 min | 3 min | 3 min | 2 min |

## 4. Yoga Poses for Kids

### Poses by Category

#### Standing Poses

**Tree Pose (Vrksasana)**
- Stand on one foot, other foot on ankle or inner calf (never on knee)
- Hands together at heart or branches reaching up
- Fun cue: "Grow your roots into the ground. Now grow your branches to the sky!"
- Easier: foot on ankle, hand on wall
- Harder: eyes closed, arms swaying like wind
- Ages: 3+

**Mountain Pose (Tadasana)**
- Stand tall, feet together, arms by sides
- Fun cue: "Stand as tall and strong as a mountain. Nothing can move you!"
- Foundation pose — use to start and return to between poses
- Ages: 2+

**Flamingo Pose**
- Stand on one foot, other foot lifted behind, arms out like wings
- Fun cue: "You're a beautiful flamingo standing in the lake!"
- Great balance challenge
- Ages: 4+

**Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I)**
- Lunge position, back foot turned out, arms reaching up
- Fun cue: "You're a brave warrior! Show me how strong you are!"
- Ages: 4+

**Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II)**
- Wide stance, front knee bent, arms extended to sides
- Fun cue: "Look over your front hand — what do you see on your adventure?"
- Ages: 4+

**Chair Pose (Utkatasana)**
- Feet together, bend knees, arms up
- Fun cue: "Sit in your invisible chair! Can you sit a little lower?"
- Ages: 3+

**Star Pose**
- Wide stance, arms and legs spread like a star
- Fun cue: "Shine like a star! Spread your points as wide as you can!"
- Ages: 2+

#### loor Poses

**Cat Pose (Marjaryasana)**
- Hands and knees, round the back up, chin to chest
- Fun cue: "Be a scared cat! Make a big round back! Hissssss!"
- Ages: 1+

**Cow Pose (Bitilasana)**
- Hands and knees, belly drops, head lifts, gentle backbend
- Fun cue: "Now be a happy cow! Belly down, head up! Moooo!"
- Ages: 1+

**Cobra Pose (Bhujangasana)**
- Lie on belly, hands by shoulders, push chest up
- Fun cue: "Slither like a snake... now rise up! Ssssssss!"
- Ages: 2+

**Downward Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana)**
- Hands and feet on floor, hips up, making an upside-down V
- Fun cue: "Be a dog stretching after a nap! Wag your tail!"
- Kids' favourite — they naturally love this pose
- Ages: 2+

**Frog Pose (Malasana)**
- Deep squat, feet wide, hands between feet or on floor
- Fun cue: "Squat like a frog... ready... JUMP! Ribbit!"
- Add frog jumps for energy release
- Ages: 2+

**Lion Pose (Simhasana)**
- Kneeling, hands on knees, big breath in, then tongue out, eyes wide, ROAR!
- Fun cue: "Take a big breath in... now let out your BIGGEST LION ROAR!"
- Amazing for releasing tension and energy. Kids LOVE this.
- Ages: 2+

**Turtle Pose (Kurmasana - modified)**
- Sit with soles of feet together, fold forward, arms under legs
- Fun cue: "Tuck into your shell! It's safe and quiet in there."
- Good for calming
- Ages: 3+

**Butterfly Pose (Baddha Konasana)**
- Sit, soles of feet together, knees out, flap legs like wings
- Fun cue: "Flutter your butterfly wings! Where are you flying to?"
- Ages: 2+

**Crocodile Pose**
- Lie on belly, hands stacked under forehead, legs long
- Fun cue: "Be a crocodile resting by the river... so still and quiet..."
- Great resting/transition pose
- Ages: 1+

**Happy Baby Pose (Ananda Balasana)**
- Lie on back, grab feet, rock side to side
- Fun cue: "You're a happy baby! Rock rock rock!"
- Natural and joyful — toddlers especially love this
- Ages: 1+

**Boat Pose (Navasana)**
- Sit, lean back, lift legs, arms forward or holding behind knees
- Fun cue: "You're rowing your boat! Row row row!"
- Sing "Row Row Row Your Boat" while holding
- Ages: 3+

**Bridge Pose (Setu Bandhasana)**
- Lie on back, feet flat, lift hips up
- Fun cue: "Make a bridge! Can a friend's stuffed animal walk under?"
- Ages: 3+

#### Yogi Club Signature Poses

**Superhero Pose**
- Stand tall, feet together, one arm raised straight up to the sky like a superhero about to take off
- Other arm can be on hip or by the side
- Fun cue: "You're a superhero! Reach your power hand to the sky! Which superpower are you charging up?"
- Great for confidence and posture
- Ages: 2+

**Flying Pose (Warrior III / Airplane)**
- Stand on one leg, lean forward, extend the other leg back and arms out to the sides like wings
- Body forms a T-shape, like flying through the sky
- Fun cue: "You're flying! Spread your wings and soooar through the clouds!"
- Easier: hands on hips, or fingertips on the floor
- Harder: arms forward like Superman
- Ages: 3+

**Super Strong Pose (Goddess Pose / Utkata Konasana)**
- Wide stance, toes turned out, bend knees into a deep squat
- Arms bent at elbows, fists up — flexing muscles like a bodybuilder!
- Fun cue: "Show me your SUPER STRONG muscles! Squeeze! You're the strongest yogi in the world!"
- Kids love flexing and showing off — this is always a hit
- Ages: 2+

**Frog Pose (Malasana)**
- Deep squat, feet wide, hands on the floor between feet
- Bounce up and down or do big frog jumps across the room
- Fun cue: "Squat like a frog... ready... JUMP! Ribbit ribbit!"
- Perfect for burning off energy
- Ages: 2+

**Rainbow Pose (Modified Side Plank / Vasisthasana)**
- Start on hands and knees
- One knee stays on the mat for support
- Extend the other leg out to the side, reach the top arm up and over in an arc — making a rainbow shape with the body
- Fun cue: "Make a beautiful rainbow with your body! Reach up and over — what colours is your rainbow?"
- Easier: keep both knees down, just reach arm up
- Ages: 3+

**Monkey Arms Pose**
- Standing with feet hip-width apart
- Swing both arms side to side, twisting the torso — like a monkey swinging through the trees
- Let the arms be loose and floppy, swinging freely
- Fun cue: "Swing your monkey arms! Ooh ooh ahh ahh! Swing to this side... and that side!"
- Great warm-up and energy release
- Ages: 1+

**Boat Pose (Navasana)**
- Sit on the floor, lean back slightly, hold behind knees
- **Option 1 (easier):** Feet stay on the floor, knees bent, lean back and balance
- **Option 2 (harder):** Lift feet off the floor, shins parallel to the ground, or legs straight up in a V
- Arms forward or holding knees
- Fun cue: "You're in a boat on the ocean! Can you balance? Don't fall in the water! Row row row!"
- Sing "Row Row Row Your Boat" while holding
- Ages: 3+

**Dino Pose (Crescent Lunge / High Lunge)**
- Big step forward into a lunge, back knee can hover or be down
- Arms up high like a dinosaur with tiny T-Rex arms, or clawed hands
- Fun cue: "You're a HUGE dinosaur! Stomp your front foot! ROAAAAR! What kind of dino are you?"
- Can do dinosaur stomps transitioning between sides
- Ages: 2+

**Warrior III (W3 / Virabhadrasana III)**
- Stand on one leg, hinge forward at hips, extend other leg straight back
- Arms reach forward (like Superman), out to sides (like an airplane), or back along the body
- Body forms one long line from fingertips to back toes
- Fun cue: "You're a warrior jet! Fly straight and strong! Don't wobble!"
- Easier: fingertips on the floor or on blocks
- Harder: arms forward, hold for 5 breaths
- Ages: 4+

#### Partner Poses

**Partner Tree**
- Stand side by side, inside arms around each other, outside feet lifted
- Fun cue: "Two trees growing together are stronger than one!"
- Ages: 4+

**Partner Boat**
- Sit facing each other, hold hands, feet touching, lean back and lift feet
- Fun cue: "We're sailing together! Don't tip the boat!"
- Ages: 4+

**Mirror Poses**
- Face each other, one leads, other copies
- Fun cue: "Be your partner's mirror! Copy everything they do!"
- Ages: 3+

**Double Mountain**
- Stand back to back, press against each other
- Fun cue: "Two mountains leaning on each other! Push gently!"
- Ages: 3+

### Breathing Exercises

**Smell the Flower, Blow the Candle**
- Breathe in through nose (smell the flower), out through mouth (blow out candle)
- Use a real flower or pretend
- Best for ages 2+

**Bee Breath (Bhramari)**
- Breathe in, cover ears, hum "bzzzzz" on exhale
- Fun cue: "Be a buzzy bee! Bzzzzzzzz!"
- Calming and fun. Ages 3+

**Snake Breath**
- Big breath in, long slow "ssssssss" on exhale
- See who can make the longest snake sound
- Ages 2+

**Balloon Breath**
- Hands around mouth, breathe in to inflate the balloon
- Spread hands wide as balloon grows
- Let go and make a "ppppbbbb" sound as balloon deflates
- Ages 2+

**Lion's Breath**
- Big breath in through nose, exhale with tongue out, "HAAA!"
- Combined with Lion Pose
- Ages 2+

**Pinwheel/Feather Breath**
- Use a real pinwheel or feather
- Practice slow, steady breath to keep it spinning/floating
- Great for teaching breath control
- Ages 2+

**Star Breath (5-Finger Breathing)**
- Spread one hand like a star
- Use other hand's finger to trace up and down each finger
- Breathe in going up, out going down
- Ages 4+

**Bubble Breath**
- Use real bubbles
- Slow, steady breath = bigger bubbles
- Teaches breath control naturally
- Ages 2+

**Ocean Breath**
- Breathe in and out through nose with a gentle "ocean" sound
- Fun cue: "Make the sound of waves inside your throat"
- Ages 5+

**Pizza Breath (for cooling down)**
- Hold up hands like holding a slice of pizza
- Breathe in to smell the yummy pizza
- Blow out slowly to cool it down
- Ages 2+

## 5. Games & Activities

### High-Energy Games (Warm-Up / Energy Release)

**Yoga Freeze Dance**
- Play music, kids dance freely
- When music stops, call out a pose — everyone freezes in that pose
- Variation: use yoga pose cards, flip one when music stops
- Ages: 2+

**Animal Parade**
- Call out animals, kids move like that animal across the room
- Bear walk, crab walk, frog jump, snake slither, bunny hop, flamingo walk
- Add music for extra fun
- Ages: 2+

**Yoga Traffic Lights**
- Green = move/dance freely
- Yellow = slow motion movement
- Red = freeze in a yoga pose
- Ages: 3+

**Yoga Obstacle Course**
- Set up stations: jump over blocks, crawl under scarves, balance on a line, do a pose at each stop
- Use yoga pose cards at each station
- Ages: 3+

**Yoga Dice**
- Large foam dice with pose pictures on each side
- Roll the dice, everyone does that pose
- Can use two dice: one for pose, one for number of breaths
- Ages: 3+

**Yoga Card Pick**
- Spread pose cards face down
- Kids take turns picking a card, everyone does the pose
- Ages: 3+

**Pose Tag**
- One child is "it" — when they tag someone, that person must hold a yoga pose for 5 breaths before rejoining
- Ages: 4+

### Calm / Focus Games

**Teddy Bear Breathing**
- Each child lies down with a stuffed animal on their belly
- Watch the teddy go up and down with breath
- "Can you make your teddy ride the wave of your breath?"
- Ages: 2+

**Birthday Candle Breathing**
- Hold up fingers as "candles"
- Blow out one candle at a time (one finger down per exhale)
- Start with 3, work up to 10
- Ages: 3+

**Statue Game**
- Music plays, kids move
- When music stops, they become "yoga statues" — hold as still as possible
- Teacher walks around looking for wobbles (gently, playfully)
- Ages: 3+

**Guided Visualisation**
- "Close your eyes. You're lying on a warm beach. Feel the sun on your face. Hear the waves..."
- Keep it simple, sensory, and short (1–3 minutes)
- Ages: 4+

**Rainbow Relaxation**
- Lie down, tense then relax each body part
- "Squeeze your toes tight like a red ball... now let go. Feel your feet melt like orange ice cream..."
- Go through rainbow colours for each body area
- Ages: 4+

**Singing Bowl Listening**
- Ring the singing bowl
- Kids raise their hand when they can no longer hear the sound
- Develops listening and focus
- Ages: 3+

### Cooperative / Social Games

**Group Pose Challenge**
- "Can everyone make one big tree together?"
- Groups of 3–5 create a pose together
- Ages: 4+

**Caterpillar Walk**
- Line up in Downward Dog, last person crawls through to the front
- Repeat until the "caterpillar" crosses the room
- Ages: 4+

**Yoga Mirror**
- Partners face each other
- One leads slow movements, the other mirrors
- Switch roles
- Ages: 3+

**Yoga Charades**
- One child does a pose, others guess the animal/object
- Ages: 4+

## 7. Behaviour Management

### Prevention is Better Than Cure

**Establish rituals and routine**
- Same opening, same closing, every class
- Kids thrive on predictability
- Use a visual schedule (pictures of each activity) on the wall

**Set expectations early**
- 3 simple rules, introduced playfully:
 1. **Safe bodies** (we don't touch others without asking)
 2. **Listening ears** (when the bell rings, we listen)
 3. **Try your best** (there's no wrong way to do yoga)
- Refer back to these consistently

**Keep them engaged**
- A busy child is a well-behaved child
- If behaviour slips, the activity probably needs to change — not the child
- Move to the next activity before you lose them
- Read the room: tired? go gentler. Hyper? add more movement first

### Common Challenges & Solutions

**The Runner (won't stay in the group)**
- Don't chase — it becomes a game
- Give them a special job: "I need a helper to hold the singing bowl"
- Create a "yoga nest" (a specific mat/spot that's their safe space)
- For toddlers: this is developmentally normal, don't stress

**The Talker (won't stop chatting)**
- Channel it: "Tell me about your dog AFTER this pose"
- Give them a leadership role: "You call out the next pose"
- Use whisper voice — they'll lean in to hear you
- "Talking stone" rule — only speak when holding the stone

**The Crier / Anxious Child**
- Never force participation
- "You can watch and join when you're ready"
- Sit them near you or their comfort person
- Offer a stuffed animal to hold
- Check in quietly: "I'm here if you need me"

**The "I Can't Do It" Child**
- "There's no wrong way! Your tree looks amazing to me!"
- Modify the pose: "Try putting your foot here instead"
- Celebrate effort, not outcome
- Partner them with a confident friend

**The Rough Player**
- Firm, calm: "In yoga, we keep safe bodies"
- Redirect energy: "Let's do 10 big frog jumps!"
- Separate from partner activities temporarily
- Speak to carers privately if persistent

**The Won't-Lie-Down Child (Savasana)**
- Don't force it — offer alternatives: seated with eyes closed, hugging a teddy
- Use a weighted eye pillow or beanbag on belly (ask permission first)
- Make it a game: "Let's see who can be the quietest sleeping bunny"
- Savasana is a skill that develops over time — celebrate small wins

### Positive Language Guide

| Instead of... | Try... |
|---|---|
| "Don't run" | "Walking feet, please" |
| "Stop talking" | "Listening ears on" |
| "That's wrong" | "Try it this way" |
| "Sit still" | "Find your mountain pose" |
| "Be quiet" | "Let's use our whisper voices" |
| "Don't touch him" | "Hands on your own body" |
| "You're not trying" | "Give it your best go!" |

### Energy Management

Reading the room is your most important skill. The class energy will tell you what to do:

**Too hyper / wild:**
- DON'T try to calm them immediately
- DO give them a big energy release first (animal walks, frog jumps, dance)
- THEN transition to breathing and calm activities
- Think of it like a wave: ride the energy up, then bring it down

**Too flat / tired:**
- DON'T push through a high-energy plan
- DO slow everything down, speak softly
- Use gentle seated poses, partner work, stories
- Extra-long relaxation

**Mixed energy (some wild, some flat):**
- Pair high-energy kids with calm kids for partner work
- Offer choice: "You can do jumping frog OR sitting butterfly"
- Use station-based activities so kids can self-regulate

## 8. Safety Guidelines

### Physical Safety

**Space:**
- Clear the area of sharp objects, hard furniture edges, small toys
- Ensure enough space between mats (arms-length apart)
- Check floor surface: not too slippery, not too hard
- If outdoors: check for glass, sticks, insects, sun exposure

**Bodies:**
- Never physically adjust a child's body without consent from parent/guardian
- Use verbal and visual cues instead of touch
- If touch is needed (e.g., placing a beanbag): "Can I put this on your belly?"
- No forced stretching — ever
- No headstands, shoulder stands, or inversions for under-6s
- Avoid deep backbends — gentle only (Cobra is fine, full Wheel is not)

**Props:**
- Check props for damage before each class
- No small parts that could be choking hazards for under-3s
- Scarves: supervise closely (strangulation risk if wrapped around neck)
- Yoga straps: not recommended for under-5s

**Allergies & Medical:**
- Always have attendance roll with emergency contacts
- Ask about allergies, asthma, injuries, or conditions before first class
- Know where the first aid kit is
- Know the centre's emergency procedures
- Keep your phone accessible for emergencies

### Emotional Safety

- Never single out a child negatively in front of the group
- Never force a child to participate
- Respect personal space — some children don't want to be near others
- Be aware of trauma-sensitive practices:
 - Avoid sudden loud noises
 - Don't insist on closing eyes (offer "soft eyes" or looking down instead)
 - Don't touch without asking
 - Always give the option to opt out
- Use inclusive language: "friends" not "boys and girls"

### Professional Boundaries

- Never be alone with a single child (always have another adult present)
- Physical contact: high-fives and fist bumps only (no hugging unless child initiates and parent present)
- Toileting: never assist — always call a centre staff member
- Photography: follow the centre's policy strictly (most don't allow phones in rooms)
- Social media: never post photos of children without explicit written consent from parents AND the centre

### Your Safety Kit (Bring to Every Class)

- Bluetooth speaker + charged phone for music
- Yoga pose cards
- Singing bowl or chime
- 2–3 scarves
- Bubbles
- 1–2 stuffed animals
- Eye pillows or beanbags (for relaxation)
- Pinwheels or feathers (for breathing)
- Attendance roll + emergency contacts
- First aid basics (band-aids, ice pack)
- Your own water bottle

### Working with Children Check (WWCC)

- **Mandatory** in NSW for anyone working with children
- Must be current and verified before you teach your first class
- Apply online: service.nsw.gov.au
- Yogi Club will verify your WWCC number
- Renewal: every 5 years

### Insurance

- Yogi Club carries professional indemnity and public liability insurance
- You are covered when teaching as a Yogi Club instructor
- Report any incidents to Giovana immediately

## 9. Working with Childcare Centres & Schools

### Before Your First Class

- Visit the centre for orientation
- Meet the room leader / teachers
- Learn the children's names (ask for a name list)
- Ask about:
 - Any children with special needs, allergies, or behavioural plans
 - The room's routine (nap time, meal time — don't clash)
 - Where you'll teach (indoor/outdoor, how much space)
 - Rules about phones, photography, sign-in procedures
 - Emergency evacuation procedures

### Building Relationships

**With centre staff:**
- Be punctual — always arrive 5–10 minutes early
- Clean up after yourself
- Share positive feedback about children: "Maya did amazing in tree pose today!"
- Be flexible if schedules change
- Remember their names and say hello/goodbye

**With parents (at pickups, if appropriate):**
- Brief positive feedback: "Liam really loved the lion pose today!"
- Never discuss behavioural concerns directly with parents (go through centre staff)
- Be friendly but professional

**With children:**
- Get on their level physically (kneel, sit on the floor)
- Learn and use their names from day one
- Remember things about them: "You told me you have a cat! Let's do Cat Pose for your cat!"
- Be consistent — same routine, same energy, same kindness every week

### Communication with Centres

- Confirm classes weekly (text or email to your contact person)
- Send a brief term plan at the start of each term
- Invoice on time (see Yogi Club invoicing process)
- Flag any concerns about children's wellbeing to centre staff (mandatory reporting obligations apply)

### Mandatory Reporting

As someone working with children in NSW, you have a legal obligation to report suspected child abuse or neglect.

**If you notice:**
- Unexplained bruises or injuries
- Sudden behavioural changes
- A child discloses abuse
- Signs of neglect (hunger, hygiene, lack of supervision)

**Do:**
- Stay calm and listen if a child tells you something
- Write down exactly what they said (their words, not yours)
- Report to the centre director immediately
- Contact the Child Protection Helpline: **132 111**
- Tell Giovana

**Don't:**
- Investigate yourself
- Question the child extensively
- Promise to keep it secret ("I'll make sure you're safe" instead)
- Discuss with other parents or staff (confidentiality)

## 11. Quick Reference Cheat Sheet

### Emergency Poses (When You've Lost the Room)

1. **REEZE DANCE** — instant engagement, works every time
2. **ROG JUMPS** — burns off wild energy in 30 seconds
3. **LION'S BREATH** — loud, fun, legitimate yoga, resets focus
4. **BUBBLE BREATH** — get out the bubbles, instant calm
5. **"WHO CAN BE THE QUIETEST MOUSE?"** — challenge = engagement

### The Golden Rules

1. **Play and have fun with kids yoga** — if they're having fun, they're learning
2. **Routine is your friend** — same opening, same closing, every time
3. **Read the room** — adapt your plan to the energy in front of you
4. **Connection before correction** — they cooperate when they trust you
5. **Music changes everything** — the right song saves the moment
6. **Shorter is better** — end while they still want more
7. **Energy flows in waves** — ride it up, bring it down, repeat
8. **You're not performing** — you're playing WITH them
9. **Plan your class, then be ready to throw the plan away**
10. **The best yoga teacher is the one who truly sees each child**

### Pose Quick List by Theme

**Jungle:** Tree, Cobra, Lion, Monkey (squat), Frog, Crocodile, Butterfly

Teaching kids yoga is one of the most rewarding things you can do. You're giving children tools they'll use for life — how to breathe when they're upset, how to feel strong in their body, how to be still when the world is loud.

The children don't need you to be perfect. They need you to be present, kind, and a little bit silly.
